Frequently Asked Questions

How do I activate sleep prevention?

Click the Awake AI icon in your Mac menu bar to open the app. Click the sun icon or toggle the switch on the Timer tab to keep your Mac awake. You can also use the global shortcut Cmd + Shift + A to toggle it from anywhere.

How do I use AI commands?

Open the app from the menu bar and click the AI tab. Type a command in plain English — for example, "Stay awake for 2 hours" or "Keep awake while an app is running" — and Awake AI will set it up automatically. AI commands require a subscription or your own API key.

Do I need an API key?

Not necessarily. Awake AI includes a free tier of managed AI commands. For unlimited AI commands, you can subscribe or enter your own API key from a supported AI provider in Settings → AI Providers → Manage API Keys.

Where is my API key stored?

Your API key is stored securely in the macOS Keychain on your device. It never leaves your Mac and is never sent to our servers.

How does App Watch work?

The Apps tab lets you add any application. When that app is running (or in the foreground, depending on the mode you choose), Awake AI will automatically keep your Mac awake. When the app quits, normal sleep behavior resumes.

What is the Battery Threshold setting?

When enabled, Awake AI will automatically deactivate sleep prevention when your battery level drops below the threshold you set. This prevents your Mac from running out of battery unexpectedly during long tasks.

How do I restore my subscription?

Open the app, go to the Settings tab, scroll down to AI Commands, and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you're signed in to the same Apple ID used to purchase the subscription.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Open System Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions on your Mac and cancel the Awake AI subscription from there. You'll retain access until the end of the billing period.

Awake AI is not keeping my Mac awake — what should I check?

First, confirm the app is active (the menu bar icon should show a sun). Check that Battery Threshold isn't triggering — if your battery is low, the threshold may be deactivating the app. Also check System Settings → Lock Screen and make sure no other system policy is forcing sleep.

How do I uninstall Awake AI?

Quit Awake AI from the menu bar (click the icon → ✕ → Quit). Then open Finder, go to Applications, and drag Awake AI to the Trash.
